Life of a Starfish

Starfish are marine animals found in oceans all over the world. They usually live on the seafloor, and they move slowly using tiny tube feet underneath their arms. Starfish can come in many colours and sizes, depending on the species and where they live.

One interesting fact is that starfish can regrow lost arms. They also eat in a unique way: some species can push their stomach out of their body to digest food outside, then pull it back in. Starfish are important to ocean ecosystems because they help keep populations of other sea creatures balanced.

Facts About Starfish

  • Starfish are not fish; they are echinoderms (related to sea urchins).
  • Most starfish have five arms, but some species have more.
  • They move using tube feet powered by a water vascular system.
  • Many starfish can regenerate arms if they are injured.
